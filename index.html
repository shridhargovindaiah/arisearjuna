Gita - Chapter 2 Verses #9 thru #15

Arjuna is in deep distress and surrenders to Krishna, seeking guidance. Krishna begins to enlighten Arjuna about the eternal nature of the soul, emphasizing that the soul is immortal and beyond physical harm. This marks the start of Krishna's teachings on the nature of life and duty.

Gita - Chapter 3 Verses #1 thru #7

Arjuna questions Krishna about the apparent contradiction between renunciation and action. Krishna explains that while knowledge is important, action is essential for maintaining the world order. He emphasizes performing one's duty without attachment to the results.

Play Now

Gita - Chapter 13 Verses #1 thru #7

Krishna begins to explain the distinction between the body and the soul. He introduces the concept of the field (body) and the knower of the field (soul), emphasizing the importance of understanding this knowledge to attain wisdom.

Play Now

Gita - Chapter 14 An introduction to the 3 GUNA's

Krishna introduces the concept of the three Gunas: Sattva (goodness), Rajas (passion), and Tamas (ignorance). These Gunas influence human behavior and understanding them is key to spiritual growth.
